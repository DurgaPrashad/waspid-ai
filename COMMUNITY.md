# Community

Waspid is a community of engineers, operators, and builders rethinking how
organizations run work — with AI workforces that are designed, deployed,
and monitored like production infrastructure.

## Mission

AI is changing how work gets done. We want that change to be something
teams can own and operate — not a black box. Waspid gives organizations the
building blocks to design AI workforces, orchestrate workflows, connect
their existing tools, and observe every run.

## Ethos

We have two core values: **high openness** and **high agency**.

### High Openness

We welcome anyone and everyone into the community by default. You don't
have to be a software developer to help us build. You don't have to be
pro-AI to help us learn.

We welcome thoughtful criticism, whether it's a comment on a PR or feedback
on the platform as a whole.

### High Agency

Everyone should feel empowered to contribute to Waspid. Whether it's by
making a PR, sharing feedback, or just asking a question, don't hold back!

Work practices, tooling, and AI capabilities are changing rapidly. We won't
hesitate to change direction and make big bets.

## Getting involved

- **Issues and discussions** — open an issue on the repository for bugs,
  feature requests, or questions.
- **Contributing** — see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for the
  development workflow.
- **Support** — write to [support@waspid.ai](mailto:support@waspid.ai).

## Upstream runtime

Waspid's agent runtime is built on an MIT-licensed open-source agent SDK.
Contributors who want to work on the agent runtime itself should engage
with that upstream project — see [CREDITS.md](CREDITS.md) for full
attribution and links.
